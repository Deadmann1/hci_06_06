2 Introduction

https://www.graztourismus.at/en is the official tourism website of the city Graz located in Austria.

The website provides a wide variety of information about different kinds of places one might want to visit in Graz. They also provide references and links to certain websites and information a tourist might need like a booking website or where to eat.

3 Evaluation Procedure

This section describes the procedure used in the heuristic evaluation.

3.1 Evaluation Methodology

Heuristic evaluation is a technique used in usability analysis to evaluate an interface design. Research shows that using this method to combine evaluations of a few evaluators (3-5) provides a better analysis of the interface and finds more problems than each evaluation on their own. [Nie1990].

Heuristic evaluations should be done by independent usability experts to prevent any bias during the evaluation and increase the quality of the report. Using a rough estimate five to eight evaluators should be able to identify around 80 percent of usability problems in the interface design. [Fard2021].

For this evaluation, the "Andrews General Usability Heuristics", shown in Appendix A.1, will be used. These are based on and slightly adapted from Nielsen's revised set of ten usability heuristics. Nielsen describes the heuristic evaluation as a cheap and easy to learn method to find usability problems in user interfaces. He further mentions that there is currently no optimum list of heuristics and each heuristic list has their merits. It is also explained that the resulting data which is the explanation of the usability problems is still very subjective and not perfectly reliable. [Nie1994a].

As mentioned in [Nie1990] it is unlikely for a single evaluator to be able to find all usability problems on his own. It has been shown that combining the independent and unbiased findings of three to five evaluators provides a very reliable evaluation which finds many usability problems. The session length of the evaluation should be limited to one or two hours as longer session can lead to mistakes. Should the interface require more time it is better to evaluate parts of the interface in multiple sessions. During the session the evaluators should not be able to communicate to ensure unbiased and independent evaluations. The goal is to inspect and evaluate all parts of the interface several times using a previously agreed on list of usability heuristics. Evaluators should describe usability problems as detailed as possible in regard to the usability principles they violated. After these session the results are aggregated in a single comprehensive report including a severity rating of each usability problem. It is also recommended to extend the heuristic evaluation by conducting a debrief session including the evaluators, any observers and the design team. The goal of the debrief session is to brainstorm designs that fix the usability problems found by the evaluation. [Nie1994b].

3.2 User Profiles

The site is specified to deliver information to tourists of Graz. But can also be useful for residents of the City.

  1. Tourists:

    1. Tourists want to get interesting spots in the city. If they already have a vague plan in their head what they want to do in Graz, they want to get specific information for that task. A good example would be that they want to visit Schlossberg. They know they want to visit Schlossberg, but don't know exactly where it is and how to get onto it. Besides, they might be interested what they can do at Schlossberg, what Restaurants are there and how long should they plan for this trip, and what they might need to bring. This was described for Schlossberg, but can be applied to any other activity.
    2. In addition they might be looking for interesting activities in the City. What is in their vacinity and how much it costs.
    3. Tourists are not just looking for fun activities just as hiking up the Schlossberg. They might want to go Shopping. They want maybe a specific shop just as Spar, but maybe also want to see what Clothing Shops Graz has to offer and where they are.

  2. Possible Tourists/Future Tourists:

    Possible Tourists are persons that might go to Graz but are not sure if this is the right city to visit for them.

    Future Tourists are persons that have decided that they want to go to Graz but they might still need information.
    1. They might look for the classic Culture or activities that are possible in Graz. Are the Music Festival, Museums etc. They want to go onto the city and see quickly in a few clicks which activities might interest them.
    2. Good hotels are often hard to find. They want to quickly get a some good recommendation. Maybe even in their target area.

  3. Residents

    Although the site is mainly targeted at Tourists. Residents can still find use out of this site.
    1. Finding new things to do. People living long and short in Graz might be interested in trying out new things or seeing what their city actually has to offer. They want to see a lot of stuff to do maybe in categories in only a few clicks.
    2. Just like 3.1.c Residents might look for good shopping possibilities in Graz.
    3. Just like in 3.2.b Residents might want to look for good Hotels for their friends or families that might want to visit Graz.
